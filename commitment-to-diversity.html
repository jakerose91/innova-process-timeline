Diversity as a performance lever

Diverse teams outperform homogeneous ones, and the reason is more mechanical than most people realise. The moral case is real and matters. The performance case is more interesting, less frequently made, and more likely to change how leaders actually hire. When a team is made up of people with genuinely different backgrounds, experiences, and ways of framing problems, the default assumptions in any discussion get challenged earlier and more often. That produces better decisions on complex, poorly-defined work, which describes most leadership work worth doing.

What we do about it

Sourcing pools

We source from outside referral networks by design. Direct outreach into communities, professional groups, and operator networks where strong candidates sit but rarely get called. The breadth of the pool determines the quality of the shortlist.

Structured assessment

Independent scoring against role-relevant criteria, recorded before the room discusses anyone. Each assessor brings their own read in writing, and consensus is built from those individual reads. Every candidate gets the same test, and capability stands on its own.

Honest framing

We tell clients when a brief or process is set up to filter for similarity. That conversation is uncomfortable, and we have it anyway. It’s where the real change happens.
